Choice of thresh in binarizing an image

When we binarize an image, the function of 0<=thresh<=1 is to promote all values \(x\ge \texttt{thresh} \) to \(1\) and send the remaining values to \(0\). The advantage with choosing lower thresh is that we get better-defined, thicker edges. However, pushing this too far can lead to the emergence of non-existent edges and noise in the image, so a balance point needs to be found empirically. As stated before, thresh=0.17 was used here.
