Convolutions of cameraman.jpg with Dx, Dy, and gradient magnitude filters

Approach Used and Results

Images to the left are original copies, the ones to the right are binarized with threshold \(h=0.17\). We are working with the first channel of cameraman.png. Given a grayscale image im, we use scipy.signal.convolve2d(im,Dx,mode='same',boundary='symm') in order to compute the convolution of our image with some filter. Observe that \(f\star D_x\) can detect vertical edges, while \(f\star D_y\) can detect horizontal edges. Finally, in order to find the gradient magnitude image, we simply compute \(\sqrt{(f\star D_x)^2 + (f\star D_y)^2} \).

Choice of thresh in binarizing an image

When we binarize an image, the function of 0<=thresh<=1 is to promote all values \(x\ge \texttt{thresh} \) to \(1\) and send the remaining values to \(0\). The advantage with choosing lower thresh is that we get better-defined, thicker edges. However, pushing this too far can lead to the emergence of non-existent edges and noise in the image, so a balance point needs to be found empirically. As stated before, thresh=17 was used here.
