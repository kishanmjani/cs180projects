Analysing the Min-Cut Contiguous Path

It is very interesting to observe the path taken by the min-cut in any of the overlapping blocks. For brick, the SSD image shows that the ssd_cost is lowest at the lines between bricks i.e. noticeable edges in the image. Thus, we observe that the min-cut path stays consistent along the dominant edges (lines between bricks), until it switches to another edge above, which it then begins to follow. Furthermore, observe that the transition occurs in a place where the pixel value changes gradually: both of these together ensure a good overlap. For brick, the path goes through a portion of the brick that is evenly red-colored. For text, the SSD image shows that the SSD cost is lowest where the text is. Subsequently, the path stays consistent along a line of text (the dominant edge), until it encounters a vertical white spacing (randomly exists because of word spacing and text alignment) wide enough that it can pass through. It is clear that this ensures low ssd_cost, and thus smooth transitions.
